# Copy Principles — Art of Living Amplify

This document is the source of truth for all marketing copy generated by Amplify. Every prompt, every channel, every piece of copy must follow these principles. Derived from competitive analysis of leading wellness brands (Headspace, Calm, Kripalu, CorePower), four copywriting books (The Copywriter's Handbook, Elements of Style, Writing Tools, How to Deliver a TED Talk), PMM Sherpa product marketing intelligence, and iterative testing against the Art of Living Happiness Program.

---

## Brand Voice

Art of Living is uplifting, dignified, warm, and grounded. The copy should sound like a friend sharing something genuinely wonderful they discovered. Not a therapist diagnosing problems. Not a salesperson pushing urgency. Not a guru lecturing from above.

**The posture:** Standing beside the reader, not above them. Sharing, not selling. Inviting, not convincing.

**The register:** Conversational but not sloppy. Simple but not simplistic. Confident but not boastful.

---

## The Five Rules

### 1. Feel, then See, then Believe, then Act

Every piece of copy follows this arc:
- **Feel:** Open with a felt experience the reader recognizes from their own life
- **See:** The Gurudev quote lands as the insight that crystallizes what they were already feeling
- **Believe:** One line of proof (millions of practitioners, 100+ studies, 40+ years)
- **Act:** A single soft CTA

### 2. Context Before Quote

Never open with a Gurudev quote. Earn it first. Set up the reader's experience in 1-2 sentences, then drop the quote as the insight that names what they were already feeling. The quote should feel like an "aha," not a sermon.

**Wrong:** "As Gurudev says, 'The deeper you are able to rest...' Sign up today!"
**Right:** "You know that feeling when you finally get to the weekend, but your body hasn't caught up?" [then the quote]

### 3. Name the Experience, Don't Diagnose It

Never tell the reader what's wrong with them. Instead, describe a moment they'll recognize. The difference between "Many people suffer from chronic stress" (diagnosing) and "You know that feeling when..." (naming). One talks down. The other walks alongside.

### 4. Understate

When the topic is serious, back off. Let the reader fill in the gap. No exclamation marks. No "life-changing." No "transform your entire existence." The more restrained the copy, the more the reader trusts it. Say less. Mean more.

### 5. Every Word Must Tell

14-16 word average sentence length. 20-40 word paragraphs. Vary sentence length for rhythm — short declarative sentences between longer flowing ones. If a word can be cut without losing meaning, cut it.

## Channel Specifications

### Email
- **Subject line:** 6-10 words. Personal, not promotional
- **Body:** 100-150 words. Three short paragraphs maximum
- **Structure:** Felt experience (1-2 sentences) → Gurudev quote → What it is + proof (1-2 sentences) → Date/location block → CTA
- **CTA:** "Find out more" or "Save your spot" — never "Register Now"

### WhatsApp
- **Length:** 60-80 words maximum
- **Tone:** As if forwarding something to a friend. No formal greeting
- **Structure:** Personal recommendation (1-2 sentences) → What it is (1 sentence) → Gurudev quote → Link
- **One link only. No images unless a clean simple flyer**

### Instagram
- **Caption:** 80-120 words
- **First line is everything** — Instagram truncates after ~125 characters. That first line must create curiosity
- **Structure:** Felt moment (1-2 lines) → Gurudev quote → What + when + where → Link in bio
- **Line breaks generously. One thought per line**
- **Image direction:** Real, not produced. Natural light. Pacific Northwest if local. No text overlay. Show life, not a retreat center

### Facebook
- **Length:** 100-150 words
- **Structure:** Felt experience or observation (2-3 sentences) → Gurudev quote → What it is + proof (1-2 sentences) → Date/location → CTA
- **Write for the nod or the share.** Provocative or resonant opening line

### Flyer
- **Headline:** 10 words or fewer
- **Body:** 50-75 words maximum
- **Structure:** Headline → Short grounding sentence → Gurudev quote → What it is (1 sentence) → Details block → CTA
- **The headline and visual earn attention. The details block answers when, where, how much**

## Quote Integration Rules

### Quality Bar
Every Gurudev quote must pass the **wall poster test:**
- Is it a COMPLETE thought? Could it stand alone on a wall?
- Does it make sense without any surrounding context?
- Is it warm, uplifting, or gently profound?
- Is it 1-3 sentences maximum?
- Are the words VERBATIM from Gurudev? Never paraphrased

### Placement
- One quote per piece of copy. Maximum. Multiple quotes become a sermon
- Place after 1-2 sentences of context that set up the reader's felt experience
- Give the quote its own line. White space before and after. Let it breathe
- Always attribute: — Gurudev Sri Sri Ravi Shankar

### Selection
- Match the quote to the channel's natural audience and emotional register
- Different quotes for different channels when possible (4-5 quotes per campaign)
- Short quotes (under 15 words) for Instagram, WhatsApp, Flyer
- Medium quotes (1-3 sentences) for Email, Facebook

## Anti-Patterns (Never Do These)

### Voice
- Never use: unlock, transform, journey, empower, elevate, harness, leverage, holistic
- Never use: emojis, hashtags in body copy, exclamation marks
- Never use: "Did you know..." or "Here's what most people don't know..."
- Never use: "Studies show..." before earning emotional permission
- Never start with the organization: "Art of Living, founded in 1981..."
- Never diagnose: "Are you struggling with anxiety? You deserve better"

### Structure
- Never lead with a quote before establishing context
- Never use multiple CTAs in one piece
- Never weave logistics (dates, pricing) into emotional paragraphs — keep them in a separate visual block
- Never warm up — start with the point. Delete warmup paragraphs from drafts
- Never produce more than one copy per channel by default

### Tone
- Never create urgency theater: "Only 5 spots left!" "Register before midnight!"
- Never overpromise: "This will change your life forever"
- Never prey on suffering or use fear/pain as a primary motivator
- Never use statistics as scare tactics (DV stats, suicide data in promotional copy)
- Never sound like you're explaining something to someone who doesn't get it

## Proof Points (Use Sparingly)

These are credibility markers to weave in naturally, not lead with:

- Practiced daily by millions in 180+ countries
- 100+ independent published studies
- 40+ years and counting
- 37% more calm in four weeks
- 60% drop in cortisol within three months
- 31% reduction in insomnia
- Over 1,000 hours of instructor training

Use at most 1-2 per piece. The proof supports the feeling, never replaces it.
