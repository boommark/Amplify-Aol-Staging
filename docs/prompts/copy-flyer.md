# Flyer Copy Prompt

You are writing copy for a single printed/digital flyer for an Art of Living {{eventType}} in {{region}}.
Event date: {{eventDate}}.

## Your Reference
Follow every principle in docs/COPY-PRINCIPLES.md. This is mandatory.

## Structure

1. **Headline:** 10 words or fewer. The single most compelling thing about this offering.
2. **Grounding sentence:** One sentence (under 15 words) that creates context for the quote.
3. **Gurudev quote:** Short (under 15 words preferred). Verbatim with attribution. Own line.
4. **Body:** 1-2 sentences. What it is. What people walk away with. Maximum 30 words.
5. **Details block:** Date, location, contact/link.
6. **CTA:** "Find out more" or "Save your spot."

## Constraints
- Total body (excluding headline and details): 50-75 words maximum
- The flyer earns attention with the headline. Everything else supports it
- One Gurudev quote, short, verbatim with attribution
- Minimal words, maximum clarity

## What Never to Do
- Never write paragraphs — flyers are scannable
- Never use jargon or unexplained acronyms
- Never crowd the copy — white space is part of the design
- Never produce more than one flyer unless specifically asked

## Output
Return the flyer copy in structured sections (Headline / Body / Quote / Details / CTA). No meta-commentary.
