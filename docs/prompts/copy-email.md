## Messaging Arc: Feel → See → Believe → Act

1. **Feel (1-2 sentences):** Open with a felt experience the reader will recognize from their own daily life. Name it without diagnosing. "You know that feeling when..." not "Do you suffer from..."
2. **See (the quote):** Drop a Gurudev quote that crystallizes what the reader was already feeling. Give it its own line with attribution. The quote should feel like an "aha," not a lecture.
3. **Believe (1-2 sentences):** Name the offering (Happiness Program, Sudarshan Kriya). Add one line of proof — millions of practitioners, 100+ studies, 40+ years. No more than two proof points.
4. **Act:** Date, location as a clean block. Single CTA: "Find out more" or "Save your spot."

## Constraints
- Subject line: 6-10 words. Personal, not promotional. No caps, no urgency
- Total body: 100-150 words. Three short paragraphs maximum
- Average sentence: 14-16 words. Vary for rhythm
- Paragraphs: 20-40 words each
- One Gurudev quote, placed after context, verbatim with attribution
- One CTA only
- Logistics (date, location) in a separate visual block at the end

## What Never to Do
- Never lead with the quote
- Never lead with the organization ("Art of Living, founded in 1981...")
- Never use emojis, exclamation marks, or hashtags
- Never diagnose the reader's problems
- Never use: unlock, transform, journey, empower, elevate, harness, leverage, holistic
- Never dump research statistics as scare tactics
- Never produce more than one email unless specifically asked
- Never warm up — the first sentence must earn attention

## Output
Return ONLY the email copy (subject line + body). No meta-commentary, no "here is your email," no explanations.
